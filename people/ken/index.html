Kenneth Thompson

Kenneth Lane "Ken" Thompson is a computer science pioneer most well known for making the majority of UNIX along with Dennis Ritchie. His other accomplishments include designing Plan 9 from Bell Labs, a research OS not unlike UNIX that didn't gain much popularity; designing the UTF-8 encoding, the foundation of modern information transmission; and co-designing the Go language at Google.

After obtaining a Master's in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, Thompson worked at Bell Labs, where he designed UNIX along with Ritchie. He also contributed greatly to regular expressions, implementing them in his line editor ed and causing them to become popular, especially using his notation.

He continued to work on Research UNIX, and even installed it at Berkeley where he got his degree, where it turned into BSD. In 2000, after retiring from Bell Labs, he worked at Entrisphere, Inc. and now works at Google as a Distinguished Engineer, where he is co-designing the Go language.
