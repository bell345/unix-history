Dennis M. Ritchie

Dennis MacAllistair Ritchie (1941-2011) was an American computer scientist most well known for his contributions to the C programming language and to the original UNIX at Bell Labs.

Ritchie began working at Bell Labs in 1967, where he helped develop UNIX with Ken Thompson and created the C programming language — a programming language used in almost every single modern computer system as the language used to build the operating system; the link between the kernel space and the user space.

Just as many modern operating systems have their roots in UNIX, quite a few modern programming languages use syntax and design elements first used in C. C++, Java, JavaScript, Python, and many more have a large part of their syntax owed to C.

Ritchie has recieved numerous awards for his work on UNIX and C, but many non-computer people haven't realised his influence on the world as we know it. He died a mere week after the death of Steve Jobs, another important computing figure, but didn't recieve nearly as much attention.
