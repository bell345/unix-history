The IBM PC threat and the fall of commercial UNIX

By the mid-to-late 1980s, the commercial UNIX community figured out that all this warring wasn't doing much to quell the threat of the IBM PC on their market. In 1988, AT&T took a 20% share in Sun Microsystems, momentarily delaying the UNIX infighting. But when IBM, DEC and other secondary vendors formed the Open Software Foundation and opposed AT&T/Sun, the fighting only continued.
