Welcome to the History of UNIX website!

UNIX is a family of multi-user operating systems first developed by employees at AT&T in the early 1970s. The practises and innovations spawned from this operating system, as well as the ones that arose during the development of its derivatives, have drastically shaped the history of computing and the basics of home computers, servers, general IT practice and all other operating systems that came after.

The UNIX trademark and the Single UNIX Specification is maintained by The Open Group.

This website chronicles the history of UNIX and its derivatives, from the early AT&T pioneers to modern usage of UNIX and UNIX-likes in everyday life. At every step of the way, helpful glossary annotations will be provided to define terms that may require a bit of elaboration.
